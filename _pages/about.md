<b>WHAT I DID IN ROBOTIC LAB</b>

I was a member of the Robotics Lab in Amirkabir University of Technology, under supervision of prof. Shiry. We worked towards preparing for Robocup Agent Simulation League. A brief summary of what this league is about: 

<i>This competition involves evaluating the effectiveness of Ambulances, Police Forces, and Fire Brigades agents on rescuing civilians and extinguishing fires in cities where an earthquake has just happened.  During rescue operations after a disaster, cooperation is a must. Participants develop the fire-brigade, ambulance-team and police-force agents’ strategies, which are algorithms. Other simulators are provided from RoboCup Rescue simulation project.</i> [[see more]](https://rescuesim.robocup.org/competitions/agent-simulation-competition/)

We participated under names: MCS and SOS-CS. Two of our TDPs (team description paper) were accepted by the international committee. You can find them [here](https://avv-va.github.io/publications/). (TDP is a paper describing the focus, ideas, and recent advancements implemented in the team.)

<b>WHAT I DO IN CAFE BAZAAR</b>

The previous summer(2019), I participated in an algorithm competition in Cafe Bazaar company. Top 30 were chosen out of the contest and I was one of them. We were accepted in the Summer Camp for software engineering in Cafe Bazaar. They taught us modern backend technologies and agile methodologies.

In Summer Camp, we worked in groups to develop projects. We used technologies such as Kubernetes, Docker, Golang, Django and Redis. It was really fun!  

After the summer camp they evaluated us and most of us were offered to work in Cafe Bazaar as a Software Engineer. So now I am currently working as a software engineer in Cafe Bazaar. We develop [bazaar](https://cafebazaar.ir/?l=en), the leading android marketplace for Persian users.
